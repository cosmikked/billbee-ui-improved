# Brand Register

Use this register when design is the product: landing pages, marketing sites, campaigns, portfolios, long-form brand storytelling, and editorial surfaces.

## Core stance

Brand work needs a point of view. Safe usually reads as forgettable. The target is distinctiveness with control, not generic prettiness.

## Typography

- Start with three concrete brand-voice words.
- Reject reflex font picks. Do not default to the usual AI-era stack of overused editorial serifs and safe geometric sans choices.
- Choose type as if the brand were a physical object: a museum caption, a terminal manual, a restaurant receipt, a poster, a packaging label.
- A single family is valid if it is chosen deliberately and handled with strong internal contrast.

## Color

- Brand surfaces can justify `Committed`, `Full palette`, or `Drenched`.
- Name the intended aesthetic lane before choosing colors.
- Palette is voice. Do not hedge a strong concept back into beige safety.

## Layout

- Use asymmetry or a visibly rigorous grid. The failure mode is the generic centered stack.
- Vary pacing across sections. Brand pages can change tempo and visual treatment as the story unfolds.

## Imagery

- If the brief implies imagery, ship imagery.
- Do not replace an obviously image-led concept with neutral rectangles or decorative UI blocks.
- One decisive image is better than five weak ones.

## Brand bans

- Monospace as lazy shorthand for "creative" or "technical"
- rounded icon-above-heading feature grids
- timid palettes and average layouts
- default editorial-magazine styling when the brief is not actually editorial
- zero imagery on briefs that clearly call for imagery

## Brand permissions

- oversized display type
- stronger color commitment
- deliberate visual risk
- section-by-section art direction
- motion that contributes to atmosphere
